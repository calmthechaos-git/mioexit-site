The 7 Best Membership.io Alternatives for 2026

You're ready to leave Membership.io but you're not sure where to go next. Here's what you'll actually pay on each platform — and the migration catch that none of them warn you about.

March 14, 2026 · 12 min read · By the MIOExit team

Every "Membership.io alternatives" article you'll find ranks Kajabi first. We're not going to do that.

Not because Kajabi is bad — it's a solid platform. But because every creator leaving MIO has different reasons, and the right alternative depends entirely on why you're leaving.

  • Leaving because of cost? Your answer is different from someone leaving because of features.
  • Leaving because you want community? Your answer is different from someone who just needs video hosting.
  • Leaving because you want control? You're building a custom stack, not shopping for another SaaS.

Here are the 7 alternatives that actually make sense — organized by the reason you're leaving.

1. BunnyCDN + WordPress — Best for Cost Control

DetailInfo
Monthly Cost$5-15/mo (storage + delivery)
Best ForCreators who want to own their infrastructure
Video HostingBunnyCDN Stream (114 global PoPs, HLS player)
Migration DifficultyMedium — requires WordPress + plugin setup

This is what we recommend to most creators who contact us, and it's what we use ourselves.

How it works: BunnyCDN hosts and delivers your videos through their global CDN. You build the front-end on WordPress using a membership plugin (MemberPress, Paid Memberships Pro, or WooCommerce Memberships) to control access. Your videos are standard MP4 files you own — no lock-in, no proprietary format.

The catch: You're building a custom stack. There's no drag-and-drop hub builder. You (or a developer) need to create the pages, organize the content, and wire up member access. It's not hard, but it's not Membership.io's "upload and go" either.

Annual savings vs. MIO: $1,100-3,400/year depending on your current MIO plan.

Real numbers: We host 2.6 TB of video on BunnyCDN. Our February 2026 bill was $0.65. The same library on MIO Pro was $299/month. See our full cost comparison.

2. Skool — Best for Community-First Creators

DetailInfo
Monthly Cost$99/mo (flat, no tiers)
Best ForCoaches and creators who lead with community
Video HostingBuilt-in (Loom-style, no AI search)
Migration DifficultyMedium — video re-upload + community rebuild

Skool is the anti-Membership.io. Where MIO is a video-first platform with community bolted on, Skool is community-first with courses built in.

Why creators switch: If your business runs on group coaching, accountability, and member interaction, Skool's gamification system (levels, leaderboards, points) drives engagement in ways MIO's static hubs can't. Members actually open the app. They post. They participate.

The catch: Skool's course/video hosting is basic. No AI-powered video search, no transcript navigation, no playlists. If your members need to find specific moments in hundreds of videos, Skool isn't the right fit.

Same price, different value: Both MIO Starter and Skool are $99/month. But Skool gives you community + courses, while MIO gives you video search + hubs. Choose based on what your members actually use.

3. Circle — Best for High-End Memberships

DetailInfo
Monthly Cost$49-399/mo (tiered)
Best ForPremium memberships with branded community spaces
Video HostingNative + external embeds supported
Migration DifficultyMedium — community setup + video re-hosting

Circle is what you choose when your membership is a brand experience, not just a course library. Custom spaces, threaded discussions, events, live rooms — all under your domain with your branding.

Why creators switch: MIO's community features are an afterthought. Circle's are the product. If you're running a premium program ($100+/month per member), Circle's polish and flexibility justify the price.

The catch: Circle isn't a video host. You'll need to pair it with BunnyCDN, Vimeo, or another video platform and embed videos into your Circle spaces. It adds a layer of complexity but gives you full control over each piece of your stack.

4. Spotlightr — Best for Video Marketing

DetailInfo
Monthly Cost$39-199/mo (tiered by bandwidth)
Best ForCreators who use video as a marketing/sales tool
Video HostingFull hosting with CTAs, gates, and analytics
Migration DifficultyEasy — direct video upload, no complex setup

Spotlightr (formerly vooPlayer) is built for creators who treat video as a conversion tool, not just content delivery. In-video CTAs, email gates, A/B testing, and engagement heatmaps — features MIO doesn't touch.

Why creators switch: If you embed course previews on sales pages, run VSLs, or use video in your funnels, Spotlightr's marketing features are leagues beyond what MIO offers. You can add opt-in forms mid-video, track where viewers drop off, and customize the player to match your brand.

The catch: Spotlightr is video-only. No member management, no community, no hub pages. You'll need a separate platform for everything else.

5. Systeme.io — Best All-in-One for Beginners

DetailInfo
Monthly CostFree tier available, $27-97/mo paid
Best ForSolo creators who want everything in one tool
Video HostingYouTube/Vimeo embeds (no native hosting)
Migration DifficultyEasy — course rebuild, but simple interface

Systeme.io is the budget all-in-one: courses, funnels, email marketing, affiliate program, and community — starting at $0/month. If you're leaving MIO because of cost and you want one tool that does everything (even if each feature isn't best-in-class), Systeme is worth looking at.

Why creators switch: The free tier includes 3 courses, 1 community, unlimited emails, and a funnel builder. For a creator just starting out or someone who was on MIO's $99 plan with a small library, Systeme can replace MIO entirely for free.

The catch: No native video hosting. You'll host videos on YouTube (unlisted), Vimeo, or BunnyCDN and embed them. The course builder is functional but basic. And the branding options are limited on lower tiers.

6. Kajabi — Best for Established Course Businesses

DetailInfo
Monthly Cost$149-399/mo
Best For6-7 figure course businesses that want one platform
Video HostingNative Wistia-powered hosting
Migration DifficultyMedium — video re-upload + course rebuild

We said we wouldn't rank Kajabi first, but we're not going to leave it off the list. If you're running a serious course business ($10K+/month revenue) and you want email marketing, funnels, checkout pages, and courses in one platform, Kajabi is the market leader for a reason.

Why creators switch: MIO is a video search platform that added membership features. Kajabi is a business platform that includes video. If you're selling courses (not just hosting them), Kajabi's marketing tools, checkout flows, and analytics give you more leverage.

The catch: It's expensive. The Basic plan ($149/month) limits you to 3 products. And Kajabi's video search is nowhere near MIO's AI-powered search. If your members rely on searching through hundreds of videos by keyword, you'll miss MIO's search.

7. Cloudflare Stream — Best for Developers and Custom Builds

DetailInfo
Monthly Cost$5/mo base + $1/1000 min delivered
Best ForTech-savvy creators building a custom platform
Video HostingEnterprise-grade streaming infrastructure
Migration DifficultyHard — requires API integration and custom front-end

If BunnyCDN is the pragmatic choice, Cloudflare Stream is the enterprise choice. Same concept (pay-per-use video hosting) but backed by Cloudflare's massive global network.

Why creators switch: If you're building a custom membership platform (React, Next.js, Django, etc.) and want rock-solid video delivery with API-first design, Cloudflare Stream is hard to beat. It's what companies like Discord and Udemy build on.

The catch: There is no UI. No course builder. No member management. This is an API and a CDN. You need a developer to build everything on top of it. For most creators, BunnyCDN is the easier version of this same approach.
