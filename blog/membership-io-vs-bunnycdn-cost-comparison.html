The Walled Garden Tax: The Hidden Cost Nobody Mentions

"I feel trapped. I don't own the files in a way that's easy to move. It's a 'walled garden'."

Membership.io's biggest hidden cost isn't on your invoice. It's what we call The Walled Garden Tax — the invisible switching cost that grows every month you stay:

  • Every video you uploaded last month? That's one more file you'll have to extract by hand — because there's no bulk download.
  • Every hub page you've carefully organized? It vanishes the day you cancel — and you'll be rebuilding it from memory.
  • Every embed link your members bookmarked? Dead. Broken. 404. The moment your MIO subscription ends.
  • And here's what nobody tells you: it gets worse every month you wait. Thirty new videos this quarter means thirty more files to extract next quarter.

With BunnyCDN, your videos are standard MP4 files stored on infrastructure you control. Want to switch to Cloudflare Stream tomorrow? Download your files and re-upload. Want to use Vimeo instead? Same thing. There's no lock-in because there's no proprietary format.

The one exception: if you have fewer than 20 videos and MIO's AI search is genuinely mission-critical for your members, the $99/month may make sense. For everyone else — especially on the Growth or Pro plan — here's what the switch actually looks like.

What the Migration Actually Looks Like

Moving from MIO to BunnyCDN isn't just "download and re-upload." Here's the real process:

  1. Audit — Catalog every file, folder, hub, and playlist in your MIO account
  2. Extract — Download all videos as MP4 files, plus transcripts (VTT/SRT)
  3. Upload — Push all files to your BunnyCDN video library with collection structure preserved
  4. Verify — Compare source and destination file counts, sizes, and playability
  5. Embed — Replace Searchie embeds with BunnyCDN iframe embeds on your site
  6. Test — Verify member access, playback quality, and transcript search
  7. Cancel MIO — Only after everything is confirmed working
